The 5 gaps
Five product gaps surfaced in the teardown.
1
Cold-start personalization
The label may overpromise before trust is earned.
2
Search intent
Search appears to rely more on keyword match than workout intent.
3
Capability onboarding
Preference is captured. Capability is only lightly inferred.

Activation friction
There appears to be too much trust cost before first workout value.

Cross-training discovery
The strategy is multimodal. Programs still appear mostly single modality.
Why it matters
All five sit at the intersection of UX, data, and sequencing.
Proof points
Finding 1
Picked for you does not yet feel fully personalized.
Observed
  • New user. Zero workout history.
  • Selected cycling, HIIT, and broad modality mix.
  • Carousel leaned recovery and stretch, with duplication.
PM lens: trust appears to be ahead of signal quality.
Metric: carousel impression to class-start conversion
Ship: honest cold start copy, coverage rules, event tracking
Finding 2
Search appears to miss workout intent when members need help most.
Observed
  • Query: cool down run.
  • Returned cycling rides rather than recovery content.
  • German content also appeared in a US English account.
PM lens: discovery appears taxonomy first, not intent first.
Metric: search to class-start conversion by intent
Ship: intent taxonomy, semantic retrieval, locale guardrails
Gap deep dive
Finding 3
Onboarding captures preference more than capability.

A 3-level self-rating may not be enough to guide intensity, progression, or recovery well.

Metric: workout-start rate after onboarding
Bet: optional movement baseline

Peloton can turn movement quality into better programming.

Finding 4
First workout value appears to arrive too late.

Health permissions, contacts, watch setup, and ad tracking may create a trust tax before members see meaningful coaching.

Metric: trial to first-workout conversion
Bet: defer asks until value is earned

This appears to be an activation problem.

Finding 5
Cross-training matters strategically, but discovery still looks weak.

Programs still appear to skew single modality, even as Peloton pushes cardio, strength, and wellness.

Metric: same-week multimodal completion rate
Bet: cross-training as first class pathway

HYROX suggests supply exists. Discovery may be the unlock.

What I would ship first
Three bets with high leverage and credible paths to ship.
Bet 1
Fix cold start recommendations
  • Honest copy before sufficient signal.
  • At least one onboarding modality match.
  • Deduplication by instructor and modality.
  • Metric: carousel impression to class-start conversion.
Bet 2
Make search understand workout intent
  • Intent taxonomy: pre, during, post, recovery.
  • Fitness synonym graph and semantic retrieval.
  • Locale filtering and language safe ranking.
  • Metric: search to class-start conversion by intent.
Bet 3
Create a true cross-training path
  • Programs category for designed cardio and floor sequences.
  • Prominent HYROX and goal based filters.
  • IQ generated weekly pairing MVP.
  • Metric: same-week multimodal completion rate.
